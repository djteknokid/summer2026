# Japan Travel Tips

Collected wisdom from experienced travelers. Apply these across the whole trip.

---

## Money & Booking

- **Free cancellation trick** — Always book hotels with free cancellation. Check the price again a few days before check-in. Prices drop regularly — cancel and rebook at the lower rate. Takes 30 seconds, saves real money.

## Getting Around

- **Suica card + eSIM** — Get a Suica card for trains and vending machines. Use Ubigi (or similar) eSIM for data. Both just work.
- **Don't pre-book trains** — No need to reserve Shinkansen in advance. Only exception: Romance Car to Hakone.
- **Stay near transit, not sights** — Shinjuku (Tokyo), Shimogyo (Kyoto), Namba (Osaka). Makes day trips much easier.

## Packing

- **Pack genuinely light** — 3 weeks in backpacks, ~5 days of clothes each. Laundry is cheap and everywhere.
- **Hotel dryers are slow** — Almost always need 2 cycles. Plan to hang clothes overnight.
- **Wear good shoes** — Non-negotiable. Your feet will betray you otherwise.

## Food

- **Tabelog > Google Maps** for food. Better reviews, easier to reserve. Most places have English menus.
- **Eat off-peak** — Late lunch / early dinner avoids the worst crowds at popular spots.
- **Convenience stores are your friend** — 7-Eleven, Lawson, Family Mart. Onigiri, sandwiches, hot food. Everywhere.
- **Many restaurants don't open until 10-11am** — Hotel breakfast is clutch for early starters.

## Daily Life

- **No public trash cans** — Carry a small bag for your trash.

## Sightseeing

- **Go early or go late** — Famous spots are a nightmare midday. Early morning or late afternoon is a different experience.
- **Quieter alternatives are often better** — Tokyo National Museum garden (empty) vs Ueno Park (insane). Houkokuji in Kamakura has a bamboo garden that rivals Arashiyama with far fewer people.
- **One day will get rained out** — Accept it. Have a backup plan: shopping, museum, onsen.

## Specific Spots

- **Nara deer** — Hide the crackers until you're deeper in the park. Deer near the entrance are aggressive. Walk in further, find a calmer spot, then feed them.
- **Hiroshima Peace Memorial** — Go early or late. Midday crowds make it hard to engage with the exhibits and survivor accounts.
- **Mt. Misen cable car** — Queue can be 1.5 hours. Go early or hike up.
